“During the day,
the light would make
this room seem a little sad,
despite the roses.”


"On the left, in a kind of recess,
there would be a large sofa
unholstered in worn black leather,
with cherrywood bookcases on either side,
heaped with books in untidy piles."


"Light would come from an old desk-lamp,
adjustable only with difficulty,
fitted with a green opaline lampshade
shaped like a visor."


"It would be May,
the start of a l o n g summer’s day."


"They would call this balance happiness

    with their freedom,
    with their wisdom
    and their culture,
they would know how to retain and to reveal it
in every moment of their living,
together."



    Georges Perec
    lists out many objects
    that are unfamiliar to someone
    like me nowadays. The way the
    chapter is structured shows him creating a setting for his characters. However, given that he doesn’t create his characters by giving them personality, it feels as if anyone could fit with these objects. I believe this feels more nostalgic, as if he’s describing his own memories rather than creating one for these unknown characters.
